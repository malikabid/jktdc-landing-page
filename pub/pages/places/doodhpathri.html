Doodhpathri

"Valley of Milk" - Kashmir's Hidden Gem

About Doodhpathri

Doodhpathri, meaning "Valley of Milk," is a pristine meadow located in the Budgam district of Kashmir, approximately 42 kilometers from Srinagar. The name derives from the milky-white appearance of the streams flowing through the valley, created by the gushing waters over limestone rocks. This relatively less-explored destination offers unspoiled natural beauty, making it perfect for those seeking tranquility away from crowded tourist spots.

Situated at an altitude of 2,730 meters (8,957 feet), Doodhpathri is surrounded by dense pine forests and snow-capped peaks. The meadow is carpeted with wildflowers during spring and summer, creating a spectacular canvas of colors. The area remains covered in snow during winter, transforming into a winter wonderland accessible only from May to October.

Main Attractions

Shaliganga Stream

The main stream flowing through Doodhpathri, known for its crystal-clear milky-white water. The stream originates from snow-fed springs and flows through meadows, creating a mesmerizing sight. Perfect spot for photography and relaxation by the waterside.

Tosa Maidan Connection

Doodhpathri serves as the gateway to Tosa Maidan, another beautiful meadow. The trek between these two destinations offers stunning Himalayan views, wildflower meadows, and encounters with local shepherds. A perfect multi-day trekking option.

Pine Forests

Dense coniferous forests surround the meadow, providing shade and enhancing the scenic beauty. The forest trails are perfect for nature walks and bird watching. In spring, the forest floor is covered with colorful wildflowers and mushrooms.

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit

May to September is ideal. The meadows are at their best in June-July with blooming flowers. Winter access is restricted due to heavy snowfall. Early morning visits offer the best light for photography and peaceful atmosphere.

Entry & Facilities

Minimal entry fee. Basic facilities available including small eateries and toilet facilities. No accommodation at the site; visitors must return to Srinagar or camp (with permission). Pony rides available for exploring the meadow.

Things to Know

  • Carry warm clothes even in summer as temperatures can drop suddenly
  • Limited mobile network connectivity; BSNL works better
  • No ATMs nearby; carry sufficient cash
  • Pack lunch or snacks as dining options are limited
  • Respect the environment; carry back all trash
  • Best for day trips; limited overnight facilities
