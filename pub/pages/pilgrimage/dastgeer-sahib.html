Dastgeer Sahib Shrine

Shrine of Hazrat Sheikh Abdul Qadir Jilani - Center of Sufi Devotion

About Dastgeer Sahib

The Dastgeer Sahib shrine, located in the Khanyar area of downtown Srinagar, is dedicated to the revered Sufi saint Hazrat Sheikh Abdul Qadir Jilani (also known as Ghaus-ul-Azam). Though the saint lived in Baghdad in the 11th-12th century, his spiritual influence spread across the world, and this shrine serves as a major center of devotion for his followers in Kashmir.

The shrine complex features beautiful Islamic architecture and is one of the most important Sufi shrines in Kashmir. The original wooden structure was tragically destroyed by fire in 2012, but it has been rebuilt maintaining its traditional architectural style while incorporating modern safety measures. The shrine continues to attract thousands of devotees who come to seek blessings and spiritual solace.

Historical & Spiritual Significance

Hazrat Sheikh Abdul Qadir Jilani (1077-1166 AD) was a renowned Islamic scholar, preacher, and Sufi saint who founded the Qadiriyya order of Sufism. His teachings emphasized devotion to God, service to humanity, and spiritual purification. Though he lived in Baghdad, his spiritual influence reached Kashmir through his disciples and followers who established this shrine in his honor.

The shrine houses a relic believed to be associated with the saint - a hair strand that is displayed on special occasions. The shrine has been a center of Islamic learning and spirituality for centuries. The tragic fire of 2012 that destroyed the original structure was deeply mourned by the entire Kashmiri community, and the rebuilt shrine stands as a symbol of resilience and unwavering faith.

Religious Practices & Festivals

Annual Urs

The main celebration held on the death anniversary of Hazrat Ghaus-ul-Azam (11th Rabi-ul-Akhir). Thousands of devotees gather for special prayers, Qawwali recitations, and spiritual gatherings. The sacred relic is displayed for public viewing.

Qawwali & Spiritual Gatherings

Regular Qawwali performances and spiritual discourses are held, especially on Thursdays and during the month of Ramadan. These gatherings attract devotees and spiritual seekers from across Kashmir.

Daily Prayers & Meditation

The shrine remains open for daily prayers and meditation. Devotees visit to offer prayers, seek blessings, and find spiritual peace in the serene atmosphere of the shrine complex.

Teachings of Ghaus-ul-Azam

Hazrat Sheikh Abdul Qadir Jilani's teachings continue to inspire millions worldwide. His key principles include:

  • Divine Love: True path to God lies through love and devotion, not just rituals.
  • Self-Purification: Cleanse the heart of negative qualities like pride, envy, and greed.
  • Service to Humanity: Serving fellow human beings is serving God.
  • Knowledge & Practice: Combine religious knowledge with practical implementation in daily life.
  • Tolerance & Compassion: Show kindness to all creation regardless of faith or background.

Visitor Information

Visiting Hours

Open throughout the day for visitors and worshippers. Best to visit outside prayer times for a peaceful experience. The shrine is especially vibrant during evening prayers and on Thursdays.

Dress Code

Modest dress required. Women should cover their heads. Remove shoes before entering the shrine premises. Maintain respectful behavior befitting a religious site.

Photography

Photography of exterior is generally allowed. Be respectful during prayer times and religious ceremonies. Always seek permission before photographing inside the shrine or during gatherings.

Facilities

Basic facilities including restrooms and ablution areas available. Langar (community kitchen) operates during special occasions. Nearby shops sell religious items and local handicrafts.

How to Reach

By Road

Located in Khanyar area of downtown Srinagar, easily accessible from Lal Chowk (approximately 3 km). Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and local buses available. Can also be reached on foot from old city areas.

From Old City

Well-connected with other old city landmarks. Walking distance from Jama Masjid and Khanqah-e-Moula. Part of the traditional old city heritage circuit.

Local Transport

Regular auto-rickshaws and local taxis available from all parts of Srinagar. Parking may be limited during peak hours and religious occasions - consider public transport or walking.

Things to Know

  • The shrine complex was rebuilt after the 2012 fire with enhanced safety features.
  • Thursday evenings see special gatherings with Qawwali performances - plan to attend for a spiritual experience.
  • The shrine is in the heart of old Srinagar - combine your visit with exploring historic bazaars and architecture.
  • During Urs celebrations, the area gets very crowded - arrive early and be patient.
  • Free meals (Langar) are distributed during religious occasions.
  • Nearby attractions include Jama Masjid, Khanqah-e-Moula, and traditional Kashmiri markets.
  • Respect the sanctity of the shrine - it holds deep religious significance for devotees.
  • The shrine represents Kashmir's rich Sufi tradition and cultural heritage.
  • Mobile connectivity may be limited in dense old city areas.
  • Carry cash as ATMs may not be readily available in the immediate vicinity.
