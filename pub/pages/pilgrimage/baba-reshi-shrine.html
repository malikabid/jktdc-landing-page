Baba Reshi Shrine

The Court Saint of Kashmir - Shrine amidst Pine Forests

About Baba Reshi Shrine

Nestled in the scenic pine forests of Gulmarg, approximately 45 km from Srinagar, the Baba Reshi shrine is dedicated to Baba Payamuddin Reshi (also known as Baba Reshi), a revered Sufi saint who lived in the 15th-16th century. Born as Payamuddin Pora, he served as a courtier in the court of King Zain-ul-Abidin (Budshah), but renounced worldly life to pursue spiritual enlightenment. He spent his later years in meditation and prayer in the solitude of these mountains.

The shrine is located on the way to Gulmarg, surrounded by dense pine and fir forests at an altitude of about 2,195 meters. The natural setting adds to the spiritual ambiance - the sound of wind through pine trees, mountain streams, and bird songs create a meditative atmosphere. The shrine is an important pilgrimage site and also attracts tourists visiting Gulmarg who stop to pay respects and enjoy the serene environment and beautiful architecture.

Historical & Spiritual Significance

Baba Payamuddin Reshi was born in the village of Pora in Kashmir in the 15th century. He was educated in Islamic theology and Persian literature and became a courtier in the court of the liberal and enlightened King Zain-ul-Abidin, also known as "Badshah" (Great King). Despite his position of privilege and comfort, Baba Payamuddin felt a calling toward spiritual life.

He renounced his courtly life and position, choosing instead the path of a Sufi ascetic. He retreated to the forests of what is now the Gulmarg area, where he spent decades in meditation, prayer, and spiritual contemplation. He became known for his piety, wisdom, and miracles. People from near and far would come to seek his blessings and spiritual guidance. His teachings emphasized renunciation of worldly desires, devotion to God, and compassion for all living beings. The shrine built at his burial site has become an important pilgrimage destination, attracting devotees who seek spiritual solace in the same forests where the saint once meditated.

Religious Practices & Festivals

Annual Urs

The main celebration held according to the Islamic calendar. Thousands of devotees from across Kashmir trek to the shrine for special prayers, Qawwali recitations, and spiritual discourses. The forested setting adds unique charm to the gathering.

Thursday Visits

Thursdays are considered especially auspicious for visiting. Devotees gather for collective prayers and meditation. The peaceful forest location provides perfect atmosphere for spiritual reflection and seeking inner peace.

Nature & Spirituality

The shrine's location allows visitors to combine spiritual practice with nature meditation. The pristine environment, fresh mountain air, and forest sounds create ideal conditions for contemplation and connection with nature.

Teachings & Legacy

Baba Reshi's life and teachings continue to inspire spiritual seekers. His key principles include:

  • Renunciation: True spiritual progress requires detachment from worldly desires and material pursuits.
  • Solitude & Meditation: Spending time alone in nature helps in spiritual contemplation and connection with the Divine.
  • Simplicity: Live a simple life free from unnecessary complications and luxuries.
  • Compassion: Show kindness to all of God's creation - humans, animals, and nature.
  • Inner Journey: The real journey is inward, toward self-realization and understanding one's true nature.
  • Service: Despite renouncing worldly life, continue to serve humanity through guidance and blessings.

Visitor Information

Visiting Hours

Open throughout the day. Best visited in morning for fresh mountain air and peaceful atmosphere. Evening visits offer beautiful forest ambiance. Combine with Gulmarg trip for a full day experience.

Dress Code

Modest dress required. Head covering recommended for women. Carry light jacket as it can be cool even in summer due to elevation. Remove shoes before entering shrine premises.

Weather

Weather can be cooler than Srinagar due to higher altitude. Summer (May-September) is pleasant with temperatures 15-25Â°C. Winter sees snowfall - shrine remains open but accessibility may be affected. Carry appropriate clothing.

Facilities

Basic facilities available including restrooms. Small shops nearby sell refreshments and religious items. Langar served during Urs and special occasions. Several restaurants and eateries on the road to Gulmarg.

How to Reach

By Road from Srinagar

Located on the Tangmarg-Gulmarg road, approximately 45 km from Srinagar (about 1.5 hours drive). Regular taxis and tourist vehicles available. Well-maintained road with scenic mountain views throughout the journey.

En Route to Gulmarg

Located about 8 km before Gulmarg, making it a perfect stop when visiting the famous ski resort. All vehicles heading to Gulmarg pass by the shrine. Can easily combine both destinations in one trip.

Public Transport

Regular bus services from Srinagar to Tangmarg (about 40 km). From Tangmarg, local taxis or shared vehicles available to the shrine and onward to Gulmarg. Affordable option for budget travelers.

Nearby Attractions

Gulmarg

Just 8 km ahead lies the world-famous Gulmarg - "Meadow of Flowers." Asia's highest cable car, skiing, snowboarding, trekking, and golf. Perfect for extending your trip to enjoy both spiritual and adventure experiences.

Pine Forests & Trails

Beautiful pine and fir forests surround the shrine. Several walking trails offer opportunities for nature walks and bird watching. Fresh mountain air and serene environment ideal for meditation and photography.

Tangmarg

Nearby Tangmarg town serves as the gateway to Gulmarg. Local market for shopping essentials, restaurants, and accommodation. Beautiful meadows and natural springs in the surrounding areas.
