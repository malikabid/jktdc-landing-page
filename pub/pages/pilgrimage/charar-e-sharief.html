Charar-e-Sharief

Shrine of Sheikh Noor-ud-din Wali - Kashmir's Patron Saint

About Charar-e-Sharief

Charar-e-Sharief is the shrine of Kashmir's most revered Sufi saint, Sheikh Noor-ud-din Noorani (also known as Nund Rishi or Alamdar-e-Kashmir), who lived in the 14th-15th century. Located in Budgam district, approximately 30 km from Srinagar, this shrine is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Kashmir, attracting devotees from all faiths and communities.

Sheikh Noor-ud-din Wali is considered the founder of the Rishi order of Sufism in Kashmir and is Kashmir's patron saint. His teachings emphasized simplicity, harmony between different religions, and living in tune with nature. The shrine complex features beautiful wooden architecture characteristic of Kashmiri craftsmanship and is surrounded by scenic saffron fields and orchards.

Historical & Spiritual Significance

Sheikh Noor-ud-din Noorani (1377-1440 AD) was a philosopher, poet, and mystic who dedicated his life to spreading the message of peace, harmony, and spirituality. His verses (known as Shruk) in Kashmiri language form an important part of Kashmir's spiritual and literary heritage. He advocated vegetarianism, simple living, and harmony between Hindus and Muslims, making him a unifying figure in Kashmiri society.

The original shrine was tragically destroyed by fire in 1995 but was rebuilt maintaining its traditional architectural style. The new structure preserves the spiritual essence while incorporating modern safety features. The shrine stands as a symbol of Kashmir's composite culture and the saint's enduring message of universal brotherhood.

Annual Urs & Festivals

Annual Urs

The main celebration held on the death anniversary of the saint (21st Zil-Hajj). Thousands of devotees from Kashmir and beyond gather to pay respects, recite the saint's poetry, and participate in spiritual gatherings.

Shruk Recitation

Special gatherings where the saint's poetry and teachings are recited and discussed. These sessions attract scholars, spiritual seekers, and devotees who find guidance in the saint's wisdom.

Daily Prayers

Regular prayer services and zikr (remembrance of God) sessions are held. Devotees visit throughout the year to seek blessings and spiritual solace at the shrine.

Teachings of Nund Rishi

Sheikh Noor-ud-din's teachings, compiled in the form of Shruk (verses), emphasize simple living, environmental conservation, and religious harmony. Some of his famous teachings include:

  • Simple Living: "Be content with a little, wear coarse clothes, and eat simple food."
  • Harmony: "Different paths lead to the same God; respect all faiths and their followers."
  • Environment: "Protect nature, for it is God's creation. Do not harm trees, animals, or water."
  • Self-Control: "Control your desires and anger; true wealth lies in contentment."
  • Compassion: "Show kindness to all living beings; see God in every creature."

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit

The shrine can be visited year-round. The annual Urs (usually in July-August) attracts the largest gatherings. Spring and autumn offer pleasant weather for visits. The surrounding saffron fields bloom in October-November.

Dress Code

Modest dress is expected. Women should cover their heads. Remove shoes before entering the shrine. Respectful behavior and maintaining sanctity of the place are essential.

Photography

Photography of the exterior is generally allowed. Be respectful when taking photos and avoid photographing during prayer times or ceremonies. Always seek permission for interior photography.

Facilities

Basic facilities including restrooms and drinking water are available. Langar (community kitchen) operates during special occasions. Parking is available near the shrine. Small shops sell religious items and local crafts.

How to Reach

By Road

Well-connected by road from Srinagar via Budgam. Regular bus services and taxis available. The journey takes approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour depending on traffic.

Local Transport

Taxis and shared cabs operate regularly between Srinagar and Charar-e-Sharief. The town is small and most locations are within walking distance from the shrine.

Things to Know

  • The shrine is open to people of all faiths - Sheikh Noor-ud-din's message was universal brotherhood.
  • The town of Charar-e-Sharief is famous for its saffron fields which bloom in autumn.
  • Traditional Kashmiri handicrafts, especially woodwork, can be purchased in local markets.
  • During the Urs, the town gets very crowded - plan accommodation and travel in advance.
  • The shrine complex includes a museum displaying artifacts related to the saint's life.
  • Local guides are available who can explain the history and significance of the shrine.
  • Maintain cleanliness and do not litter within the shrine premises.
  • The architecture showcases traditional Kashmiri craftsmanship - observe the intricate wooden work.
  • Nearby attractions include Yusmarg, Doodhpathri, and other Budgam district destinations.
  • Many devotees distribute free food and tea to visitors, especially during religious occasions.
