Martand Sun Temple

Ancient Marvel of Kashmir's Architectural Heritage

About Martand Sun Temple

The Martand Sun Temple, also known as Martand Temple, stands as one of the most magnificent examples of ancient Kashmiri architecture. Built in the 8th century AD by King Lalitaditya Muktapida of the Karkota Dynasty, this temple complex was dedicated to Surya (the Sun God). Located near Anantnag, about 64 km from Srinagar, the temple sits majestically on a plateau offering panoramic views of the Kashmir Valley.

Despite being partially destroyed during the medieval period, the temple's grandeur remains evident in its massive stone structures, intricate carvings, and sophisticated architectural design. The temple showcases a unique blend of Gandhara, Gupta, and Chinese architectural styles, making it an exceptional specimen of Kashmir's rich cultural heritage.

Dedicated To Surya (Sun God)
Architectural Style Kashmiri temple architecture with Greek and Gandhara influences

Best Time to Visit April to October

Architectural Features

  • Central Shrine: The main sanctum was designed with 84 columns in a colonnaded courtyard
  • Stone Construction: Built entirely of massive limestone blocks without any mortar
  • Trefoil Arches: Distinctive Kashmiri architectural element visible throughout the structure
  • Intricate Carvings: Elaborate sculptures and reliefs depicting mythological scenes
  • Strategic Location: Built on a plateau to align with the sun's path
  • Courtyard Design: Surrounded by 84 chambers for priests and devotees

How to Reach

By Road

Regular taxi and bus services available from Srinagar. The temple is well-connected by road and can be reached via NH 44.

By Air

Nearest airport is Sheikh ul-Alam International Airport, Srinagar (64 km away).

Visiting Information

Opening Hours Sunrise to Sunset (All days)
Entry Fee Free
Photography Allowed
Duration 1-2 hours

Travel Tips

  • Visit during early morning or late afternoon for best lighting and fewer crowds
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes as the terrain is uneven
  • Carry water and sun protection, especially during summer months
  • Allow time to explore the entire complex and enjoy panoramic valley views
  • Consider hiring a local guide for detailed historical insights
  • Combine visit with nearby Achabal Gardens and Verinag Spring
