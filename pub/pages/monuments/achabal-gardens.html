Achabal Gardens

About Achabal Gardens

Achabal Gardens, also known as Achabal Bagh, is a stunning Mughal garden located in the Anantnag district of Kashmir. Built in 1620 AD by Empress Nur Jahan, the wife of Emperor Jahangir, this garden is renowned for its natural springs that cascade down in beautiful waterfalls and fountains. The name "Achabal" is derived from "Achha-Baal" meaning "good water" in reference to its abundant natural springs.

The garden exemplifies the Mughal tradition of creating paradise on earth through the harmonious integration of water, architecture, and landscaping. The crystal-clear spring water flows through the garden in multiple channels, creating a symphony of sounds and a refreshing atmosphere. The garden features three terraces adorned with Chinar and cypress trees, vibrant flowerbeds, and traditional Persian-style pavilions. It served as a royal retreat and continues to enchant visitors with its serene beauty and natural splendor.

Garden Features

  • Natural Springs: Abundant crystal-clear spring water throughout the garden
  • Cascading Waterfalls: Multiple levels with spectacular water features
  • Three Terraces: Beautifully landscaped ascending levels
  • Royal Pavilions: Traditional Mughal architecture and pavilions
  • Chinar Trees: Ancient Chinar trees providing shade and beauty
  • Flower Gardens: Vibrant seasonal flowers and manicured lawns
  • Water Channels: Network of channels carrying spring water
  • Fountains: Traditional Mughal fountains throughout the garden

How to Reach

By Road

Regular bus and taxi services from Srinagar and Anantnag. Well-connected via NH 44.

By Air

Sheikh ul-Alam International Airport, Srinagar is 58 km away.

Travel Tips

  • Best visited during spring and summer when flowers are in full bloom
  • The sound of cascading water creates a peaceful, meditative atmosphere
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes to explore all three terraces
  • Visit during weekdays to avoid crowds and enjoy the serenity
  • Combine with nearby Verinag Springs and Kokernag for a complete garden tour
  • The garden offers excellent photography opportunities, especially the waterfalls
