Kokernag

The Land of Perpetual Springs

About Kokernag

Kokernag is a scenic hill station located in the Anantnag district of Kashmir, renowned for its pristine natural spring that is the largest in Kashmir. The spring discharges millions of gallons of crystal-clear water daily, forming the source of the Veshaw River, a major tributary of the Jhelum. Set amidst lush gardens developed during the Mughal era and expanded by the British, Kokernag offers a serene retreat with terraced lawns, colorful flower beds, and towering chinar trees.

The name "Kokernag" is derived from "Koker," meaning hen, and "Nag," meaning spring, referring to the spring's sound resembling a hen's clucking. The garden, spread over 4 hectares, features a unique water channel system that distributes the spring water throughout the terraced levels. With its trout-rich streams, botanical garden, and cool climate even in summer, Kokernag serves as a perfect picnic spot and nature retreat for families and nature enthusiasts.

At a Glance

Location Anantnag District, 70 km from Srinagar
Altitude 2,133 meters (7,000 feet) above sea level
Garden Area 4 hectares (10 acres) of terraced gardens
Best Time to Visit April to October, especially May-June for flowers
Temperature Summer: 18-28°C, Winter: -2 to 10°C

Main Attractions

Kokernag Spring

Kashmir's largest natural spring with crystal-clear water flowing at millions of gallons per day. The spring feeds beautiful garden pools.

Botanical Garden

Terraced gardens with diverse flora including rare flowers, medicinal plants, colorful roses, and towering chinar and willow trees.

Trout Fish Hatchery

Government-run trout hatchery established in 1960s. Visitor-friendly facility where you can observe trout breeding and life cycle.

Water Channels

Intricate Mughal-era water channel system distributing spring water through gardens, creating cascades and pools at different levels.

Nature Trails

Walking paths through surrounding forests of pine and deodar, offering glimpses of Himalayan biodiversity and birdlife.

Scenic Viewpoints

Multiple vantage points within gardens offering panoramic views of snow-capped peaks, valleys, and the Veshaw River gorge.

Activities & Experiences

Garden Walks

Stroll through terraced gardens, enjoying the spring water sounds, colorful flowerbeds, and peaceful atmosphere.

Picnicking

Perfect family picnic destination with manicured lawns, shaded areas under chinar trees, and children's play areas.

Photography

Capture spring waters, terraced gardens, mountain backdrops, colorful flowers, and reflections in clear pools.

Trout Fishing

Angling permitted in designated areas of Veshaw River with proper permits from Fisheries Department.

Nature Therapy

Experience therapeutic benefits of pristine spring water, fresh mountain air, and tranquil natural surroundings.

Short Treks

Easy trails to nearby viewpoints and through surrounding forests, suitable for all age groups and fitness levels.

Visitor Information

Entry Fee

₹15 per person for adults, ₹5 for children. Additional charges for vehicle parking (₹20-50).

Timing

Gardens open daily: 8:00 AM - 7:00 PM (Apr-Oct), 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Nov-Mar). Best visit: morning or late afternoon.

Duration

2-3 hours for garden exploration and spring visit. Half day including nearby Achabal Gardens and Verinag.

Facilities

Basic refreshment stalls, washrooms, parking. Better dining options in Kokernag town (3 km) or Anantnag (26 km).

Accommodation

Tourist bungalow near gardens. More options in Anantnag town. Day trip possible from Srinagar or Pahalgam.

Accessibility

Lower garden levels wheelchair accessible. Upper terraced areas have steps. Family-friendly with children's play area.

How to Reach

By Road

70 km from Srinagar (2 hours via NH44). 26 km from Anantnag town (40 minutes). Regular buses and shared taxis from Anantnag. Private taxis available from Srinagar. Well-connected paved road.

Nearest Airport

Srinagar International Airport (70 km, 2 hours drive). Hire taxi or arrange hotel pickup for convenient transfer to Kokernag.

Nearest Railway

Jammu Tawi Railway Station (300 km). From Jammu, take bus/taxi to Srinagar or direct to Anantnag, then proceed to Kokernag.

Things to Know

  • Wear comfortable walking shoes for exploring terraced gardens
  • Carry water bottles, snacks, and sun protection (hat, sunglasses)
  • Best photography during golden hours (early morning and late afternoon)
  • May-June offers best flower blooms; avoid monsoon season (July-Aug)
  • Spring water is potable and considered medicinal by locals
  • Combine visit with nearby Achabal Gardens (20 km) and Verinag (45 km)
  • Fishing permits required from Fisheries Department for angling
  • Gardens can be crowded on weekends and public holidays
  • Respect garden rules - no littering, plucking flowers, or feeding fish
  • Winter visits offer snow-covered scenery but limited garden blooms
  • Carry light woolens even in summer - evenings can be cool
  • Suitable for all ages including elderly and children
