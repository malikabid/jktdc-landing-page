Khanqah-e-Moula

Kashmir's First Khanqah - Shrine of Mir Syed Ali Hamadani

About Khanqah-e-Moula

Khanqah-e-Moula, also known as Shah-e-Hamadan mosque, is one of the most revered Islamic shrines in Kashmir. Located on the banks of the Jhelum River in old Srinagar, it is dedicated to Mir Syed Ali Hamadani (1314-1384 AD), a Persian Sufi saint and scholar who played a pivotal role in spreading Islam and establishing Islamic culture in Kashmir. This historic khanqah (Sufi monastery) is Kashmir's oldest Muslim shrine and a masterpiece of traditional Kashmiri architecture.

The building is an architectural marvel, constructed entirely of wood with intricate papier-mâché work, beautiful calligraphy, and traditional Kashmiri craftsmanship. The shrine has survived multiple fires throughout history and has been rebuilt each time by devoted followers, maintaining its original architectural style. The serene location on the Jhelum riverbank and the spiritual ambiance make it one of Srinagar's most significant cultural and religious landmarks.

Historical & Spiritual Significance

Mir Syed Ali Hamadani, known as Shah-e-Hamadan (King of Hamadan), was a renowned Persian Sufi saint, scholar, poet, and propagator of Islam. He visited Kashmir three times between 1372 and 1384 AD, bringing with him 700 Sayyids (descendants of Prophet Muhammad) who were scholars, craftsmen, and mystics. His arrival marked a significant turning point in Kashmir's history, as he laid the foundation of Islamic culture, introduced various crafts (including shawl-making and papier-mâché), and established educational institutions.

The Khanqah was built at the site where the saint is believed to have stayed during his visits. It became the center of Islamic learning and Sufi practices in Kashmir. Shah-e-Hamadan's teachings emphasized moral living, devotion to God, and service to humanity. His influence on Kashmiri society was so profound that he is often credited with shaping Kashmir's Islamic identity while promoting harmony and peaceful coexistence. The shrine has withstood numerous fires (1479, 1731, 1994, 2010) but has been rebuilt each time with the same dedication and craftsmanship.

Architectural Marvel

Wooden Construction

Built entirely of deodar wood without using any nails. The traditional Kashmiri "Taq" system of interlocking wooden beams demonstrates ancient engineering brilliance. The craftsmanship has been preserved through centuries.

Papier-mâché & Calligraphy

Exquisite papier-mâché work adorns the interior walls and ceiling with intricate floral patterns and geometric designs. Beautiful Islamic calligraphy featuring Quranic verses enhances the spiritual atmosphere.

Traditional Design

Features a distinctive Kashmiri pyramidal roof, ornate wooden pillars, and intricate carvings. The riverside location with reflection in Jhelum waters adds to its ethereal beauty, especially at dawn and dusk.

Religious Practices & Festivals

Me'raj-un-Nabi

One of the most important celebrations at Khanqah-e-Moula. Commemorates the Prophet's night journey and ascension. Special prayers, recitations, and gatherings attract thousands of devotees. The shrine is illuminated beautifully.

Shah-e-Hamadan's Urs

Annual commemoration of Mir Syed Ali Hamadani's death anniversary (6th Dhul Hijjah). Week-long celebrations include spiritual discourses, Qawwali performances, and distribution of food to the needy.

Daily Prayers & Gatherings

Five daily prayers congregated by devoted followers. Thursday evenings see special gatherings. Ramadan brings heightened spiritual activity with Taraweeh prayers and Quranic recitations attracting large crowds.

Shah-e-Hamadan's Contributions

Mir Syed Ali Hamadani's influence on Kashmir extended far beyond religion:

  • Spiritual Guidance: Established Sufi practices and Islamic learning centers across Kashmir.
  • Crafts & Industries: Introduced shawl-making, papier-mâché, woodcarving, and other crafts that became Kashmir's identity.
  • Social Reforms: Promoted ethical living, cleanliness, and social welfare programs.
  • Education: Established madrasas (educational institutions) for religious and secular education.
  • Literature: Authored numerous books on Islamic jurisprudence, Sufism, and ethics still studied today.
  • Harmony: Advocated peaceful coexistence and respect for all communities.
  • Economic Development: The crafts he introduced became major economic drivers for Kashmir.

Visitor Information

Visiting Hours

Open throughout the day for prayers and visitors. Best visited between morning and evening prayers for peaceful exploration. Avoid visiting during congregation prayers unless participating. Photography sessions best in soft morning or evening light.

Entry Restrictions

Women are not allowed inside the main prayer hall as per Islamic tradition. Women can visit the exterior, courtyard, and designated areas. All visitors must maintain appropriate decorum and respect.

Dress Code

Modest dress mandatory. Head covering required for women visiting the premises. Men should avoid shorts. Remove shoes before entering. Maintain silence and respectful behavior in prayer areas.

Photography

Photography of the magnificent exterior and architectural details permitted and encouraged. Interior photography requires special permission and should be done discretely without disturbing worshippers. Drone photography prohibited.

How to Reach

By Road

Located in old Srinagar city center, approximately 3 km from Lal Chowk. Auto-rickshaws and taxis easily available. Accessible via the old city roads running along Jhelum River. Part of old city heritage circuit.

Walking from Old City

Walking distance from Jama Masjid (about 1 km). Can be combined with walking tour of old Srinagar's heritage sites including wooden bridges and traditional bazaars. Pleasant riverside walk available.

River Route

Located on Jhelum riverbank - can be viewed from river during shikara rides. The reflection of the shrine in river waters, especially during sunset, offers stunning photo opportunities. Ask boatmen to take you nearby.

Things to Know

  • One of Kashmir's most important historical and religious monuments - UNESCO heritage consideration pending.
  • The shrine has survived multiple devastating fires but has been rebuilt each time maintaining original design.
  • Best viewed from across the Jhelum River to appreciate its full architectural beauty and riverside setting.
  • During Me'raj celebration, the shrine and surrounding area are beautifully illuminated - spectacular sight.
  • The papier-mâché work inside is considered some of the finest in Kashmir - worth seeing if permitted.
  • Combine visit with exploration of old city including Jama Masjid, traditional markets, and wooden bridges.
  • The area around the shrine has narrow lanes - be careful of traffic if walking.
  • Local shops sell religious items, traditional Kashmiri handicrafts, and books on Shah-e-Hamadan.
  • Ramadan brings special spiritual atmosphere with Iftar (breaking fast) served in the complex.
  • The shrine represents Kashmir's rich Islamic heritage and craftsmanship traditions.
  • Respect the sanctity - this is an active place of worship with deep religious significance.
  • Best photography: early morning mist over Jhelum with shrine reflection, or sunset golden hour.
  • Parking is limited in old city - consider walking or using auto-rickshaws.
